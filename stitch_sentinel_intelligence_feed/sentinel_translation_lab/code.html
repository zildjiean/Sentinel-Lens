Advanced Persistent Threat Group 'Zodiac' Targets Critical Energy Infrastructure via Zero-Day Vulnerability

Recent telemetry gathered from edge nodes in Northern Europe indicates a coordinated campaign by a threat actor currently identified as Zodiac. The group is leveraging a previously undocumented zero-day vulnerability (tracked internally as SL-2024-089) within industrial control systems to maintain persistent access.

The attack vector involves a sophisticated phishing operation targeting high-level systems administrators. Upon successful compromise, the payload executes a memory-resident RAT (Remote Access Trojan) that bypasses standard heuristic-based detection layers.

Technical Insight

"The payload exhibits behavior consistent with state-sponsored activity, specifically in its avoidance of disk-level writes and use of encrypted C2 channels over HTTPS."
